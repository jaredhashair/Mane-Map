Before Your Appointment

Know before you go.

Most guys walk into their first consultation not knowing what to ask. Here's what you actually need to understand beforehand — so you show up like you've done this before.

Understanding base types

Thin skin poly — most natural-looking hairline, best for exposed styles like slicked back or brushed up. Easier to clean, takes adhesive well. Not as breathable — heavy sweaters may notice heat. Lasts 2–3 months.

Thicker poly (0.06–0.08mm) — better for obscured or partially exposed hairlines like forward, tousled or messy styles. Lasts 4–5 months.

Lace / Hybrid — most breathable and comfortable. Best for active lifestyles and heavy sweaters. Lasts 4–5 months. Needs more care than poly.

Active + heavy sweater → lace. Exposed hairline style → thin skin poly. Obscured style → thicker poly.

Your first 24–48 hours

Give your bond 24–48 hours to fully set before any water exposure — no showers, no swimming, no sweating through an intense workout.

A shower cap is your best friend for the first couple of days. After that you're good to go.

Week one reality check

The first week is the hardest — not physically, just mentally. You'll check the mirror constantly looking for the line. You'll wonder if people can tell. They can't. But your brain hasn't caught up yet.

By week two most guys stop looking for it and just see their hair. The guys who struggle longest are the ones who walked in without realistic expectations. Now you have them.

Questions to ask your specialist

  • What bases do you specialize in?
  • What adhesive do you use and why?
  • Do you work with systems the client brings in?
  • What does the maintenance schedule look like?
  • Can you walk me through basic home maintenance?
  • What's the realistic replacement timeline?

A specialist who welcomes these questions is the right one.

What maintenance actually looks like

Partial DIY — the most common route. Salon installs your new system, you handle maintenance at home. Most guys do a full removal, clean, and reinstall every 10–14 days. About 1.5 hours including the shower. By month three it's muscle memory.

Full salon — you show up, they handle everything. No maintenance knowledge required. Simpler, but it adds up. For a lot of guys that tradeoff is worth every dollar.
